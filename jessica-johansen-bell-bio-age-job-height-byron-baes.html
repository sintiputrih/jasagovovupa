Meet Byron Baes' Jessica Johansen-Bell! Her Bio, Age, Job, Height

Netflix’s first Australian Reality Docu soap Byron Baes certainly revolved around a lot of controversies, but Jessica Johansen-Bell couldn’t care less. “I felt like the town would move on from it,” she said.

Let’s take a deeper look into her life as this Jessica Johansen-Bell Bio proceeds.

Jessica Johansen-Bell On Byron Baes

Netflix’s controversial reality show Byron Bay premiered on March 9, 2022, and there stood Jessica Johansen-Bell among 13 other influencers who helped each other grow their businesses, connections, and social followings in the Australian beach town of Byron Bay.

Explaining the show, Nathan Favro (a participant) said that the casts were a “tight crew of content creators” while Cai Leplaw labeled the coastal town “a mecca for influencers.”

Well, the theme was quite clear.

But while the show is undoubtedly addictive, many Twitter critics described Byron Baes as “cringeworthy” and “brain-numbing.”

“Byron Baes is horrific. But I will watch the entire thing,” a fan wrote.

Also, this wasn’t Byron Bay’s first criticism. Back when the show was still being filmed, locals began raising their voices in “alarm, disgust, and outrage” that the production flouted filming and environmental protocols. They alleged that the show presented a glossy and false impression of the place and cast out-of-towners as locals.

Producer, Paul Franklin acknowledges the local’s anger but shared they “got a lot of positive feedback too.”

Likewise, Jessica feels the same. “It wasn’t going to be a life-threatening situation. People might be angry about it and then see it, and maybe not be angry,” she explained.

As for the question, if the show’s scripted or not — two participants Saskia Wotton and Sarah Tangye, vouched that the show was 100% organic.

Joining Jessica in the inaugural season of Byron Bay were co-stars — Elle Watson, Sarah Tangye, Jade Kevin Foster, Simba Ali, Elias Chigros, Nathan Favro, Alex Reid, Dave Frim, Saskia Wotton, Hannah Brauer, Cai Leplaw, Elle Watson, and Lauren Johansen Bell.

Happy to have made memories on Byron Bay, Jessica wrote over IG on Feb 23, “So lucky to be able to create these memories with my sister by my side.”

Jessica Johansen-Bell Relationship Status

Jessica Johansen-Bell was in a relationship with his boyfriend named Adam when she appeared on Byron Bay in 2022.

They two were in a relationship before Jan 2017 and stayed strong for over 5 years.

Her boyfriend, Adam was often the star of her IG, and he was even the one who first let her know the town Byron Bay won’t be happy about the show. “My partner said to me multiple times, “The town’s going to lose it over this”, but they often protest a lot of things so I wasn’t too concerned,” Jessica recalled.

Jessica Johansen-Bell Job

Jessica Johansen-Bell is the owner of a clothing brand titled “Johansen.” She founded the company back in 2015 at Byron Baym, first offering chic, flattering silhouettes, made from luxurious fabrications.

But was only in 2018, her company blossomed after the billionaire cosmetics mogul Kylie Jenner shared multiple photographs of her wearing Johansen clothes on her IG.

Back then, Kylie wore The Corset Jumper dress in white, which sold out immediately.

A dress fanatic, Jessica started designing unofficially when she was 16. According to her, she was always cutting up her clothes and altering them. So, after finishing high school, Jessica then immediately started her job at Island Lux (high-end homeware, fashion, and accessory boutique).

And within six months of joining the boutique, Jessica founded Johansen.

However, Jessica continued working at Island Lux for 3 years. Also, it was at the very boutique where she first displayed her pieces.

“It blew up from there and I had my first small pop-up store in the back alley behind Island Luxe,” she recalled.

The last we checked, Johansen’s latest arrival collection included Naomi Gown, Kate Mini, Tyra Bandage Dress, running around $300.
