Christopher Scott Ellis Biography – Christopher Scott Ellis Wiki

“Kentucky” Christopher Scott Ellis (Chris Ellis) was best known by his nickname “Kentucky” on Discovery Channel’s Street Outlaws. He was a cast member on the inaugural season of “Street Outlaws” in 2013 and continued to make appearances as a mechanic over the years. He last appeared on a 2018 episode.

According to Christopher Scott Ellis’ obituary, “Chris excelled in working with his hands at multiple trades, but eventually settled on a career as a street-racing auto mechanic. He became known for his dedication to his job and his quick sense of humor at Midwest Street Cars Automotive in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma.”

Chris also enjoyed hunting and spending time outdoors, according to his obituary. His obit reads, “He was generous and kind, and knew exactly how to make the people he loved smile. Chris was a dearly-loved brother, son, and friend who will be deeply missed by all who knew him.”

Born in Cynthiana, Kentucky, he was raised in Pendleton County and graduated from the Log Cabin Learning Center in Morgan, Kentucky.

Christopher Scott Ellis Age

Christopher Scott Ellis was born on November 7, 1980, in Cynthiana, Kentucky. He died on September 9, 2020, at the age of 39.

Christopher Scott Ellis Death

Christopher Ellis was found dead at his home in Oklahoma City on Wednesday, September 9, 2020. He was 39 years old.

Christopher Scott Ellis Cause of Death

Christopher Scott Ellis’ cause of death was an apparent heroin overdose, according to a police report obtained by TMZ.

Christopher Scott Ellis Family

Chris Ellis is survived by his mother Loleta Sullins and his six siblings; three brothers Robbie Ellis, Quentin Ellis, and Logan Justice; along with his three sisters, Amanda Scothorn, Alana Messer, and Amanda Whobrey.
