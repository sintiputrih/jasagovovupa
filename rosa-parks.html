Biography

Rosa Parks was born in Tuskegee, AL on February 4, 1913. Civil rights activist who became known as “The first lady of civil rights” after she was arrested for refusing to give up her seat on a bus in 1955. This act instigated the legendary Montgomery Bus Boycott, which helped end racial segregation on public transit across the country. She worked with Martin Luther King Jr., who led the Civil Rights movement. Her school, the Montgomery Industrial School, was burned down twice by racist arsonists.
