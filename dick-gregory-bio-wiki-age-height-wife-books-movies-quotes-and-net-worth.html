Dick Gregory Biography

Dick Gregory was an American comedian, vegetarian activist as well as civil rights. He was born and brought up in St. Louis, Missouri, the United States as Richard Claxton Gregory. His writings were the best sellers. He became well known among the African-American communities in the Southern United States through his “no-holds-barred” sets, poking fun at bigotry as well as racism in the United States.

Dick Gregory Education

Gregory earned a track scholarship to Southern Illinois University (SIU), where he set school records as a half-miler and miler. In 1954, his college career was interrupted for two years when he was drafted into the United States Army.

At the urging of his commanding officer, who had taken notice of his penchant for joking, Gregory got his begin in comedy within the Army, wherever he entered and won many talent shows. In 1956, Gregory, in brief, came back to SIU when his discharge however born out as a result of him feeling that the university “didn’t wish Maine to check, they wished Maine to run.
