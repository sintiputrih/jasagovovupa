Early Life and Career

Let’s start by introducing Steve Harvey. Born on January 17, 1957, Harvey is an American television host, producer, actor, and comedian. He has had a successful career hosting various shows such as Judge Steve Harvey, the Family Feud series, Celebrity Family Feud, Family Feud Africa, and the Steve Harvey Morning Show. Additionally, he has presided over the Miss Universe pageant. Throughout his career, Harvey has received numerous awards and recognition, including fourteen NAACP Image Awards, two Marconi Awards, and seven Daytime Emmy Awards.

Harvey first started his career as a stand-up comedian in the 1980s. He then hosted Showtime at the Apollo and starred in The Steve Harvey Show on The WB. In 2000, he appeared in The Original Kings of Comedy after performing on the Kings of Comedy Tour. Harvey has been the host of Family Feud and Celebrity Family Feud since 2010. He has also hosted shows such as Steve Harvey’s Funderdome and Little Big Shots Forever Young. As a four-time published author, Harvey’s most popular work is “Act Like a Lady and Think Like a Man,” which was released in March 2009. In 2017, he established Steve Harvey Global, a media company that includes his production company East 112 and various other projects. He also launched an African version of Family Feud and contributed to Anthem Sports and Entertainment’s acquisition of HDNet.

Philanthropy and Personal Life

Apart from his professional endeavors, Harvey is also known for his philanthropic efforts. He and his wife Marjorie founded The Steve and Marjorie Harvey Foundation, a nonprofit organization that focuses on youth education. Harvey has a total of seven children, four biological and three stepchildren, from three different marriages.
