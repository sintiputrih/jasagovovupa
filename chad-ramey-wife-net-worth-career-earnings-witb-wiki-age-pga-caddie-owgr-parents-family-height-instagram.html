Chad Ramey Biography – Chad Ramey Wiki

Chad Ramey is an American professional golfer who plays on the PGA Tour. He joined the Korn Ferry Tour in 2016 and the PGA Tour in 2022. He earned his first Korn Ferry Tour tournament win at the Live and Work in Maine Open in June 2021.

The former Mississippi State golfer turned professional in 2014. He officially earned his first PGA Tour card by finishing in the top-25 of the 2020-21 Korn Ferry Tour regular season points standings. He finished the 2020-21 combined season No. 4 in the Korn Ferry Tour points standings after recording a win at the 2021 Live and Work in Maine Open, 11 top-10 finishes, 24 top-25 finishes and 40 made cuts across 43 starts.

The Fulton, Mississippi native was a University of Mississippi fan growing up and went on to attend Mississippi State University. He graduated with a degree in Insurance, Risk Management, & Financial Planning from Mississippi State in 2014. He played for the Mississippi State men’s golf program from 2010-14.

In his Junior Season (2012-2013), he won his first tournament as a Bulldog after beating the field with a 3-under, 213 at the Tiger Invitational. He was named a First Team All-SEC member following his successful junior campaign, becoming the first Bulldog to garner the honor since 2000.

Prior to MSU, Ramey was a USGA Junior Amateur qualifier after a first place finish in a tournament in Murfeesboro, Tenn. and the champion at the Mississippi Boys Junior Amateur in Gulfport, Miss. He was Individual state champion for three years in a row (2007-10) and was a part of three state championship efforts for Itawamba Agricultural High School (2007-10).
