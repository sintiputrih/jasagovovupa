Ken Harrelson Biography

Ken Harrelson, nicknamed (The Hawk) due to his distinctive profile, is an American retired professional baseball All-Star first baseman and outfielder, who played in Major League Baseball (MLB) from the year 1963 to 1971. He was born on September 4, 1941, in Woodruff, South Carolina, the United States as Kenneth Smith Harrelson.

Ken is most widely known for his 33-year tenure as a broadcast announcer for the Chicago White Sox. In December 2019, he was named the 2020 recipient of the Ford C. Frick Award, granted annually to one broadcaster for (major contributions to baseball).
