Susan Lucci, 76, Looks 'Even More Beautiful Now' than When She Played in 'All My Children,' Fans Say

Susan Lucci is a successful actress best known for her notable performance in ABC's Soap Opera "All My Children," which aired for over four decades from 1970 until 2013.

The veteran actress, now 76, recently shared a throwback photo on Instagram that left her fans in awe. The picture, which Lucci described as one of her favorite photoshoots, is a portrait made for Erica Kane's (Lucci) character from "All My Children."

A role in which Lucci received many recognitions for her impressive performance. The throwback photo sparked reactions from fans, more so the ones who keenly followed the program.

One fan commented, "You're even more beautiful now than you were then." Another fan said the photo was cute and complimented the actress for being beautiful inside and out. She then congratulated Lucci for her impressive role in the drama series that saw her win an Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress.

The fan then praised Susan Lucci, stating that she read her biography and got more insight into the actress' life. She continued, "You're a loving and caring person and love how supportive your late husband Helmut was of u."

At 76 years, Lucci is hotter than ever. Her youthful look has left fans wanting to know about her beauty tips and skincare routine. The actress once revealed that her commitment to her skincare routine has contributed to her ageless look.

She stated that no matter how tired she can be, she never retires to bed with her makeup on. After carefully removing her makeup, she washes her face with a facial cleanser, then applies Serum with growth factors.

Lucci also opened up about having done Botox procedures and complimented her Doctor for doing a great job that left her looking natural. While Lucci keeps fit and sticks to her beauty tips, the mom of two believes aging is a state of mind and body and doesn't define beauty.

Susan Lucci was born in December 1946. She has Italian, Swedish, German, and French roots. Though she has appeared in several movies and TV series, such as "Devious Maids," her role in "All My Children," in which she costarred alongside Kelly Ripa, Mark Consuelos, and Sarah Michelle Gellar, is considered her most notable performance.
