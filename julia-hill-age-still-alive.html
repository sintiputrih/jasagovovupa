Who is Julia Hill?

Julia Butterfly Hill, born on February 18, 1974, is an American environmental champion and advocate for redirecting taxes.

Her most renowned feat was her 738-day stay in a colossal 200-foot-tall California redwood named Luna, which stood for about 1000 years.

From December 10, 1997, to December 18, 1999, she lived in Luna to halt loggers from the Pacific Lumber Company who intended to fell the ancient tree.

Through determined efforts, Hill successfully negotiated with the lumber company to spare Luna.

She shared her remarkable journey in the 2000 book “The Legacy of Luna” and co-authored “One Makes the Difference.”

Living in the Tree

One of the most remarkable chapters in Julia Hill’s life is her tree-sitting activism.

In December 1997, she climbed a 180-foot-tall redwood tree named Luna, located in Humboldt County, California.

Determined to prevent its logging, she decided to live in the tree for over two years, until an agreement was reached to spare it from destruction.

Hill’s tree-sitting act gained widespread media attention and became an iconic symbol of peaceful environmental resistance.

Her protest not only protected Luna but also brought global attention to the issue of deforestation and the importance of preserving old-growth forests.
